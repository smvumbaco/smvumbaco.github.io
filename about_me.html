About Me

I am currently a 4th year undergraduate student at Northeastern University studying Electrical and Computer Engineering. I have worked at Analog Devices and Teledyne FLIR on individual devices and components as well as robotic systems. On campus, I work as an ECE career peer and serve as a technical lead in the student-led Generate Product Development Studio.

My Resume

I've had a passion for Electrical and Computer Engineering ever since I designed a high school AP Research project where I developed a long-term voltage monitor using solar cells and a simple Arduino Uno setup. At Northeastern, I've been able to expand my skills significantly through coursework in circuit design and programming as well as clubs and personal projects relating to product development. Most notably, I am heavily involved with Northeastern's Generate Product Development Studio, which develops student-led prototypes designed to bring to market. Over the last few years, I've gained a broad understanding of PCB design and fabrication, system test, working with an interdisciplinary group of engineers, among other invaluable skills. I showcase a bit of this professional and project experience in my Portfolio, which demonstrates some of my engineering experiences so far!

Some of my personal hobbies include playing piano and bass guitar with my band, skiing, hiking, and mountain biking. I'm heavily involved with the Catholic Center at Northeastern, and I take part in intramural sports such as ultimate frisbee. I'm a big fan of popular culture media such as fantasy and sci-fi movies and TV. While I haven't been able to travel that much on a college engineering student's budget, I have big plans to travel to Europe and South America in the future!
