Generate: Chief Engineer

During my time at Northeastern, I had the honor of working as Generate Product Development Studio's Chief Electrical Engineer. This position was the culmination of my time at Generate, where I had previously worked as an Electrical Hardware Engineer and Electrial Technical Lead. In the Chief role, I oversaw the electrical development of 4 prototypes at a time, assisting as a mentor throughout the entire engineering design process. I also presented workshops where I taught about a variety of topics ranging from component selection to semiconductor physics. In addition, I wrote written design reviews following formal PDR and CDR presentations from each team.

The main purpose of workshops was to forward the professional development of Generate's members. This includes learning physics and theory, preparation for technical interview questions, and hands-on activities, among other formats. Beyond professional development, we placed a large emphasis on engagement, and frequently found creative formats to present what might otherwise be mundane information. For instance, we turned technical interview preparation into a competitive game of jeopardy, where teams competed against one another to earn prizes.

Like workshops, design reviews took a variety of formats. To give members public speaking experience, teams performed formal PDR and CDR presentations. My role in these presentations was to scrutinize and question their designs, offering advice and pointing out potential pitfalls in their work. Design reviews also took a written form; teams would submit their projects through GitHub and I would write up formal documents critiquing and complimenting their work. This way, members had a reference to draw upon as they made changes to their circuits.
