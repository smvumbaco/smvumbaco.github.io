Emerald Innovations: Device Assembly

My work at Emerald exposed me to several different sides of a small business. While I spent much of my time at Emerald working on my hard technical skills, I undertook a variety of tasks that enhanced my soft skills instead. Chief among these tasks was assembling health monitoring devices. While this responsibility did little to forward my engineering ability, it greatly improved my buiness acumen.

To assemble devices that would eventually be shipped to participants, I had to keep close track of inventory and restock supplies when needed. I had to maintain an extremely keen attention to detail, as a single mistake in the flashing process would cause extreme damage to the company's ability to collect and transmit data. I kept to a strict schedule, paying attention to when new devices were required and having them ready to ship in a timely manner. Finally, I held a keen sense of my contributions to the business; the devices I built went straight into participants' homes to collect data, allowing the business to maintain existing contracts and make new ones.
