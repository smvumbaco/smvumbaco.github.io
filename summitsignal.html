Capstone: SummitSignal

SummitSignal is a portable and lightweight SOS beacon and GPS data tracker I developed as a member of a six-student team for our Engineering Capstone project. SummitSignal is targeted for individuals who engage in any kind of outdoor activity that could take place outside of cell service such as hiking or sailing. This device utilizes satellite tracking to record the user's location data and transmit that data to emergency services in case of an emergency. For this project, I collaborated on the circuit design process and was chiefly responsible for the backend development and AWS integration of our web application.

Much of the hardware involved in this project revolves around the custom PCBA. This prototype is designed with three major sybsystems: power management, MCU control, and GNSS/Iridium communication. The schematic and initial footprint for this project are shown below.

Due to budget constraints, this board was hand-soldered, so ICs such as the Teensy 4.1 MCU and the IMU were connected to the PCB via development boards. Still, the module was designed to be user-friendly and roughly the size of an iPhone. Transmitting data through satellite communication was made possible by the external RockBLOCK module, while receiving coordinate data was possible through the integrated GNSS antenna on the board.

The dataflow of the backend web development is shown below. The device operates in two modes. The first mode, shown to the left, sends the user's location and other important information to emergency contacts via the Iridium satellite system and AWS services such as EC2 and SNS. The second mode passively records user location data and processes it using AWS services such as Cognito and DynamoDB. This tracking mode was added so the user can view information about their outdoor excursions later on.
