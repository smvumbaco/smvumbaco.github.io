Generate: WaveWise

WaveWise is an ocean farming venture seeking to simplify and commercialize ocean farming of kelp. The assembly consists of a surface buoy and a submersible, depth-adjustable buoy connected by a tethering system. Each buoy supports its own electrical system and uses custom PCBs. The system uses sensor readings of water temperature, salinity, pH, UV levels, and pressure to determine whether to raise or lower the farming platform to optimize plant growth. The design incorporates custom PCBs and cables as well as extensive embedded development. On this team of four mechanical engineers and six electrical/computer engineers, I served as the Electrical and Computer Engineering Technical Lead where I drove development, mentored the other EE/CEs, and facilitated important design decisions.

The surface buoy is responsible for power generation and includes a solar battery charging system. Most of the power generation is required to power the stepper motor, which uses a pully system to raise and lower the kelp lines in the water. The buoy communicates with the submersible via a specialized cabling system and the UART communication protocol. The submersible, in turn, carries and interprets the data of several different sensors before sending the data to the buoy system, which responds by adjusting the depth of the submersible in the water and storing the data in a microSD card.

For this project, I had the priviledge of overseeing a group of five Electrical and Computer Engineers, each with different strengths and interests. Beyond developing the system, I was also responsible for delegating work and serving as the final say in design and purchase decisions.
