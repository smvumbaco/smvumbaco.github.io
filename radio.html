MFJ-9317 Radio

During my last semester at Northeastern, I assembled, tuned, troubleshooted, tested, and operated the MFJ-9317 ham radio. This radio operates at 18MHz, a very high speed that introduced several design challenges. Beyond the high frequency, faulty components and limited resources resulted in the need for creative solutions, such as breaking out a mixer and soldering it to contact points via leads, as shown below.

Assembling the PCB involved soldering through-hole components onto the PCB, winding inductors used in the H-filter, tuning the variable-frequency oscillator to operate at 8MHz (the radio maintains an unchanging intermediate frequency of 10MHz), tuning the beat-frequency oscillator to operate at about 10.0006MHz, and adjusting the Tx/Rx bandpass filters to propagate a narrow band centered at 18MHz. Finally, the device was hooked up to speakers, a hand key, and antennas before being used to transmit and receive tones to a similar radio. This entire process is detailed in the report included below.
